How Constructor differs from sponsorship

A sponsor buys visibility. A Constructor buys a team. 8 contracted pros, 1 coach, a 5-year operating mandate, local-market revenue rights, and a seat at the table for league-level decisions. Sponsorship is advertising. Constructor is franchise IP — with a 4.9× probability-weighted MOIC on the $10M fee ($5.6M op cash + $22M media halo + $17.5M Y5 sellable franchise = $45M combined).

A4 · Sponsorship Activation Rubric

HOW SPONSORS SHOW UP AT EVERY STOP

Festival-weekend activation, not static signage.

Every P1 stop is a 4-5 day festival weekend. Sponsor activation is architected in layers — arena branding, festival zones, broadcast integration, and Constructor joint campaigns. Static signage is the floor, not the ceiling.

FESTIVAL ZONES

Branded activation footprint

Each stop includes dedicated festival-weekend zones for title + category sponsors. Tasting, sampling, product demo, play-zones. Attendance 4-5k per weekend, with brand dwell time 45-90 min per attendee.

VENUE BRANDING

Arena + on-court inventory

4-5k seat arenas. On-court logo placements, broadcast visible. Concourse activation. Digital signage. Naming rights on individual courts within a stop (e.g. "adidas Center Court").

BROADCAST INTEGRATION

Content inventory within feeds

Title sponsor lower-thirds, category sponsor commercial breaks, kit sponsor on-player visibility, named segments within docuseries + highlight shows. Every broadcast hour has structured inventory.

CONSTRUCTOR JOINT CAMPAIGNS

Team-level activations

Sponsors at category tier can run joint campaigns with a specific Constructor team. Audience crossover between global brand marketing and team-specific fan base. Athlete-led creative opportunity.

DIGITAL + CREATOR

Always-on amplification

Sponsor-licensed clip inventory for social + creator content. Named social series ("Monster Moments of the Week"). Paid commercial rights on P1 content catalog to sponsors.

GRAND SLAM PREMIUM

Tentpole moments

4 Grand Slams per year are premium activation windows. Extended broadcast, higher foot traffic, VIP guest circuits, trophy presentation placements. Premium pricing layer on category + title tiers.

B1 · Advisory Retainer Scope
PAGE 10
WHAT ASSAY RETAINS

Quarterly cadence. Annual offsite. Board-observer access.

Advisory retainer scope is deliberately narrow: strategic, not operational. Cadence-driven, not retainer-billed-by-hour. Single point of accountability to the P1 President plus Concorde-aligned investor relationships.

QUARTERLY REVIEW

90-minute strategic session

Every quarter. Topline metrics review, Constructor recruitment + retention, media-rights status, sponsorship pipeline, governance health. Pre-read distributed 7 days prior.

60-DAY STRATEGIC PLAN

Written deliverable each quarter

Forward-looking 60-day plan authored by Assay. Shipping priorities, risk flags, market-condition update, recommended decisions for the President. Standing format, standing cadence.

ANNUAL OFFSITE

2-day strategic retreat

Board + senior leadership + Assay. Year-ahead planning. Scenario stress-test. Constructor roadmap, IPO timeline, governance-evolution milestones. Shared strategic memory.

BOARD OBSERVER

Non-voting board access

If mutually agreed: Assay sits as board observer through tracking-stock IPO. Attends every board meeting. Access to all materials. No vote, no fiduciary — informational + advisory only.

INVESTOR LIAISON

Anchor + co-investor interface

Assay retains relationship with anchor investor post-close. Maintains thesis alignment, flags portfolio-synergy opportunities, keeps anchor expectation management current.

LIGHTWEIGHT ADVICE

Unscheduled check-ins

Informal 15-30 minute calls on demand for strategic questions between quarterly sessions. No hourly billing; folded into annual retainer.

What's out of scope

Not included in the retainer: operational management, day-to-day Constructor relationship management, media-rights selling, sponsorship selling, legal / HR / finance functions. Assay is strategic partner, not fractional operator. Operational work is handled by P1's in-house team + Ramsports.

B2 · Live-Escalation Retainer
PAGE 11
ON-CALL WHEN IT MATTERS

48-hour SLA on crises that affect the category.

The quarterly cadence carries the business in normal state. The live-escalation layer covers the moments that determine how P1 gets talked about: Constructor disputes, integrity events, media crises, anchor-facing emergencies. 48-hour response SLA, prioritized over non-P1 engagements.

TRIGGER EVENTS

When Assay is on-call

  • Constructor dispute escalating to legal / PR threshold
  • Integrity event (player suspension, betting probe, doping inquiry)
  • Media crisis — negative broadcast-partner or press narrative at Grand-Slam scale
  • Anchor-facing concern (covenant risk, governance dispute, Concorde amendment)
  • Regulatory intervention (Olympic committee, sports-law, cross-border work-permit)
RESPONSE PROTOCOL

48-hour SLA

  • Initial acknowledgment within 2 hours, any timezone
  • Initial strategic memo within 48 hours, written deliverable
  • Working daily until the event closes; cadence defined by severity
  • Post-event debrief within 14 days — lessons captured into skill / playbook update
  • Playbook update captured into P1 governance library for future reuse

B3 · Governance Touchpoints
PAGE 12
WHERE ADVISORY MEETS GOVERNANCE

Where Assay participates in league-level decisions.

Advisory is non-voting. But there are specific governance touchpoints where Assay's strategic role is formally referenced — Concorde amendments, Ramsports board nominations, anchor-investor liaison, and pre-IPO preparation.

CONCORDE AMENDMENTS
Any amendment to the Concorde Agreement (governance terms, anchor rights, Constructor obligations) is reviewed by Assay prior to board vote. Advisory memo attached to every amendment ballot.
RAMSPORTS BOARD NOMINATIONS
Assay consulted on any replacement or addition to the Ramsports board / advisory bench. Consulted on governance-credibility dimension — not veto, but formal input.
ANCHOR-INVESTOR LIAISON
Assay maintains relationship with anchor investor from Concorde signing through tracking-stock IPO. Formal liaison role for portfolio-synergy activation and thesis-alignment maintenance.
PRE-IPO PREPARATION
Assay leads pre-IPO strategic narrative development. Works with P1 legal + bankers on S-1 strategic sections, investor-day messaging, analyst-day preparation. Hand-off to ECM team at roadshow.
CONSTRUCTOR SEAT RESALE
If a Constructor seat returns to Ramsports for resale, Assay consulted on replacement brand screening. Preserves governance-credibility and anchor portfolio alignment.
SERIES B + PRE-IPO ROUNDS
Advisory memo on each subsequent capital round. Dilution analysis, strategic-fit review of new LPs, preferred-partner identification.
Advisory is not governance

Assay does not hold voting rights, has no fiduciary obligation, and does not participate in executive decision-making. The role is explicitly advisory — strategic input, not operational control. Voting authority rests with the anchor, the Constructor bench, Ramsports, and the P1 board.

B4 · Advisory Pricing Structure
PAGE 13
FIXED RETAINER + PERFORMANCE COMPONENT

$500k-$1M annual retainer. 1% of advisory-driven revenue.

Pricing aligned to outcomes. Fixed retainer covers quarterly cadence + escalation SLA. Performance component captures upside when Assay-driven relationships or deals close. No hourly billing, no fee creep.

BASE RETAINER
$500k - $1M / yr

Fixed fee, quarterly billed

Covers quarterly cadence, 60-day plans, annual offsite, board-observer access, 48-hour escalation SLA. Range reflects scope tier — $500k at minimum engagement, $1M with full board-observer + pre-IPO scope.

PERFORMANCE LAYER
1% of incremental

Of Assay-driven revenue deals

1% of incremental revenue from deals directly attributable to Assay-driven introductions or advisory (new Constructor seat sold via Assay intro, media partner sourced by Assay, etc.). Applied only to net-new deals above baseline, verified quarterly.

What's excluded

  • No hourly billing. Quarterly cadence is fixed; escalations folded into retainer.
  • No success-fee on anchor close. Anchor investor relationship predates Concorde; not Assay-driven for pricing purposes.
  • No performance on media-rights deals in renewal cycles. Only on net-new media deals sourced by Assay introduction.
  • No double-dipping with legal / banker fees. If a transaction has fee coverage elsewhere, advisory performance does not stack.
Term + termination

Initial term: 3 years from Concorde close (2026-2029), covering pre-IPO window. Renewal at market. Termination for convenience with 90-day notice. Termination for cause (material breach) immediate. Hand-off obligations included: materials, relationships, and strategic memory transfer to internal / successor team.
