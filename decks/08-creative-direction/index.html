01 · Brand Thesis
PAGE 02
THE POSITIONING ANCHOR

Institutional. Confident. First-principles.

P1 is not a pickleball-league brand — it is the institutional commercial-rights holder of the category. The creative posture must match that reality. Every asset, every caption, every trailer must read as though it came from the organization that writes the sport's rules, signs its media deals, and builds its docuseries. Not from a tournament promoter.

THE MODEL

F1 + LIV + Drive-to-Survive cinema-ready

  • F1 — institutional scale, category authority, broadcast cadence
  • LIV — category-vacuum confidence, willingness to name incumbent failure
  • Drive-to-Survive — character-first, rivalry-led, cinema-grade docuseries
  • Not MLS circa 2005. Not the PPA. Not SaaS-adjacent.
NOT THIS

Three traps to actively avoid

  • SaaS aesthetic — gradient-blob startup energy. P1 is not a product launch.
  • Frat-bro sport — hype language, shouty copy, locker-room hedonism.
  • Retail-sport shoutiness — exclamation-point PR voice, "most exciting!" superlatives without receipts.
The one-sentence test

Would this caption, image, or trailer look at home on the F1 feed, the LIV Golf feed, and Formula 1: Drive to Survive in the same scroll? If yes, it's on-brand. If it reads as louder, cheaper, or more casual than those references, it's off.

EIGHT ATTRIBUTES · FOUR ANTI-ATTRIBUTES

How P1 sounds in every caption, headline, and trailer.

Voice Attributes

Institutional
Builder of the category

Speaks as the body that organizes the sport — not as a fan or a sponsor.

Declarative
States, doesn't hedge

"Season 1 launches February 2027." Not "We're hoping to launch Season 1."

Numerate
Numbers as nouns

20 stops. 17 markets. $50M player pool. Numbers lead, adjectives trail.

Cinematic
Shot, not staged

Every visual reads as though it came from a season-arc docuseries, not a brand campaign.

Scarce
Eight seats. Four slams.

Constraint language. Scarcity is the product. The voice reflects it.

Grounded
Real names. Real places.

Proper nouns over generics. "Miami Grand Slam" not "the US opener."

Global
Not US-shaped

Dubai, Singapore, Madrid, Sydney are all default locations — not outposts.

Receipt-backed
Every claim cites a source

No vibes-based assertions. Every number traces to the canonical financial model.

Anti-Attributes · Actively avoid

Hype-heavy
"Insane" · "Crazy" · "Electric"

Adjective inflation signals amateur. P1 lets the numbers and footage do that work.

Apologetic
"We're just excited to..."

Softening openers, hedged posture. P1 is the institutional body, not a supplicant.

Retail-casual
Emoji-forward · trend-voiced

Marketing-team-on-TikTok energy. Breaks the institutional frame instantly.

Techno-jargon
"Platform" · "Ecosystem" · "Synergies"

SaaS vocabulary. Always replace with concrete nouns: league, event, broadcast, Constructor.

03 · Narrative Arcs
PAGE 04
FOUR SPINE STORIES · TOLD REPEATEDLY

The brand tells these four stories — and no others.

Every piece of long-form content (docuseries, investor memo, press release, keynote) pulls from one of four narrative spines. Repetition is the point. Audiences encode a brand by hearing the same four stories in different contexts.

ARC 01

The Bartlett Principle

Pickleball is exponential, not incremental. 75M global model base → 300M by 2032. Three of every four 2032 players have not yet picked up a paddle. The institutional category is pre-competitive.

  • Anchored in Albert Bartlett's exponential-function quote
  • Frames "why now" in every investor-facing asset
  • Repeats across cover slide, macro-context slide, video opener
  • Numbers read as receipts, not forecasts
ARC 02

The Vacuum Thesis

The incumbent (PPA / UPA / MLP) is broken on four axes: governance conflict, player resentment, closed media, U.S.-only frame. P1 is built from the opposite philosophy on each. Not a reform — a replacement.

  • Names the incumbent failure without apology
  • Maps each failure to a P1 structural answer
  • Anchors the "why P1, why not them" part of every call
  • Repeats across governance deck, master pitch, press posture
ARC 03

The Participation Flywheel

Spectators become players. Players become consumers. Consumers fund the next event. Festival-weekend design is the accelerant — 4-5 day stops where hospitality, concerts, celebrity play, and pro matches compress the conversion funnel into a single visit.

  • Links Sideline's participation-sports thesis to P1 mechanics
  • Anchors Constructor pitch ("your consumers become our audience")
  • Docuseries thread — fan who becomes a competitor
  • Connects brand ROI to category compounding
ARC 04

The Concorde Architecture

Governance before capital. Three bodies. Escrow until milestone. Full refund if 8 Constructors not secured by July 1, 2026. The F1 lesson, ported on day zero. This is the credibility moat.

  • Frames every institutional-capital conversation
  • Answers the "what if this fails" question upfront
  • Docuseries thread — the signing of the Concorde
  • Repeats across governance deck, investor strategy, master pitch

04 · Docuseries Framework
PAGE 05
F1 DRIVE-TO-SURVIVE ANALOG · CHARACTER-FIRST · SEASON-ARC

The creative bible for P1's flagship content.

Pillar 1 — Character-First

Storylines over scores

Drive-to-Survive works because audiences follow drivers, not teams. P1's primary unit is the player. Episodes are built around 2-3 players per swing, not around event recaps. Scores are evidence, not plot.

  • Player backstory before match footage
  • Off-court intimacy (family, training, rivals)
  • Loss-narrative as honest as win-narrative
Pillar 2 — Season Arcs

10 episodes. 1 championship.

10-episode season follows the 4-swing calendar toward Dubai Tour Championship. Mid-season pivot (Episode 5-6) centers on a rivalry or Constructor crisis. Finale resolves season-long arc.

  • Episode 1: the Miami Grand Slam opener — stakes established
  • Episodes 2-4: swing-by-swing, player arcs layered
  • Episode 5-6: turn — rivalry / Constructor war-room
  • Finale: Dubai Tour Championship · season resolution
Pillar 3 — Rivalries

Tension is structure

Every season needs 2-3 defined rivalries. Player vs player. Constructor vs Constructor. Coach vs coach. Rivalries give audiences a reason to return — they are the most cited driver of DTS audience retention.

  • Verbal tension captured on-camera is gold
  • Off-court friendship + on-court rivalry = prestige layer
  • Rivalries must be authentic — not manufactured
Pillar 4 — Constructor War-Rooms

The boardroom is content

Drive-to-Survive's team-principal interviews are the prestige mechanic. P1 has the same: Constructor President strategy sessions, Ramsports integrity calls, commercial-rights negotiations — all fair game when captured with access.

  • Ramsports stewardship meetings (redacted where integrity-sensitive)
  • Constructor war-rooms pre-event
  • Commercial-rights conversations (under NDA bracket)
Distribution posture

Year-2 onward. Target: one-season exclusive to a single streaming platform (Netflix, Apple TV+, Amazon Prime Video). Rights sell per-season, not per-league. Run-rate projection $15-30M/yr by Y3. DTS comp: estimated $50M/season windfall for F1 post-Liberty.

Production doctrine

All four formats share the same visual grammar: institutional color palette, Outfit display, grounded camera work. No floating graphics, no hype-cut editing. Sound design draws from F1 / Break Point — not from streaming-highlight TikTok culture. Creators and fans filming on phones are part of the pipeline; their footage is invited into the official channel under a credit structure.

06 · Cultural Positioning
PAGE 07
FESTIVAL-WEEKEND · GLOBAL-CITIZEN · PARTICIPATION-LUXURY

Where P1 sits in the cultural landscape.

Festival-Weekend Aesthetic

Not "event" — gathering

  • Four to five days, not two afternoons
  • Pro play + celebrity Fridays + concerts + food markets
  • Families through day, adults through night
  • Visual language: Coachella-meets-Wimbledon, not PPA stands
Global-Citizen Frame

17 markets as default

  • Copy mentions Dubai, Singapore, Madrid without "international" framing
  • No US-exceptionalism, no translator energy
  • Host cities are equal footing with the league
  • Docuseries captures global arrivals as baseline, not novelty
Cross-Cultural Posture

Asia-first sensibility

  • Ramsports leadership anchored in China / HK — shapes visual + operating language
  • Grand Slams rotate hemispheres — no US primacy
  • Language parity: English lead, with subtitle / companion copy in host language
  • Hospitality doctrine = Asia-resort, not US-stadium
Participation-Sport Luxury

Access + aspiration

  • Adjacent to Athletic Brewing / Bandit / UGP / OLIPOP positioning
  • Active-leisure audience — not couch-sport audience
  • Viewers become players; players become viewers
  • Luxury cue: the event itself, not the signage
The cultural claim

P1 sits at the intersection of institutional sport (F1), festival gathering (Coachella / ACL), and participation-consumer luxury (Bandit / UGP / Athletic Brewing). No competing league occupies this three-way intersection. The aesthetic choices across every asset must reinforce the intersection, not collapse into any single corner of it.

07 · Voice Do / Don't
PAGE 08
TEN SAY-THIS-NOT-THAT EXAMPLES

The brand's voice, contrasted precisely.

Don't say Say
"The most exciting new league in sports." "P1 launches February 2027. 20 stops. 17 markets. $50M player pool."
"Team sponsorship opportunity" "Constructor seat — 8 global brands, $10M license, 5-year renewable."
"Prize money" "Player pool" (includes salary feed + event prize + bonuses — $50M+ combined).
"Our exciting media rights" "Global commercial rights — media, sponsorship, broadcast, licensing."
"Tournament" "Stop" (standard) or "Grand Slam" (quarterly anchor) or "Tour Championship" (finale).
"Investment opportunity" "Anchor stake in the global commercial-rights holder. $50M at $250M+ implied."
"League officials" "Ramsports stewards" (on-site) or "Ramsports integrity office" (post-event).
"Pickleball is growing fast!" "24.3M U.S. participants. 75M global model base. 300M 2032 scenario."
"Our ecosystem of teams" "Eight licensed Constructors — scarce by design."
"We're excited to announce..." "Announcing —" (drop the softener; state the fact).
The pattern

The "say" column always replaces adjectives with numbers, hype with structure, and common sport nouns with the P1-specific terminology. Every term on the right carries institutional weight — it identifies the speaker as P1, not as a third-party observer.

09 · Crisis-Comms Posture
PAGE 10
SCANDAL · SUSPENSION · INTEGRITY VIOLATION

The brand posture when things go wrong.

Every institutional sport will eventually face one of three categories of crisis: a player scandal, a player suspension, or an integrity violation. The creative doctrine for each is the same — transparency, Ramsports independence, player-first remedy. The worst posture is the one the incumbent uses (deny, delay, deflect). P1's posture is the opposite.

Posture 1 — Transparency
Ramsports publishes findings same-day. Written steward ruling, reasoned decision, appeal path clearly stated. No "no comment" period. No embargo on facts established. The audience learns what happened from P1, not from a leak.
Posture 2 — Ramsports Independence
All public integrity statements come from Ramsports — not from the Investor entity, not from the Constructor, not from League Operations. The independence of the regulator is reinforced in the reporting line and the voice of the statement. "Ramsports has ruled..." not "the league has decided..."
Posture 3 — Player-First Remedy
Where a sanction is warranted, the written ruling includes the remedy path: appeal window, reinstatement conditions, support resources (medical / psychological where applicable). Players are treated as members of the system, not adversaries of it. This is the explicit inverse of PPA's "culture of fear."
Posture 4 — Constructor Reputational Separation
Constructor takes the reputational hit on its own player's conduct, but is not joined to the Ramsports process. Official statements distinguish cleanly between team-side accountability and league-side sanction. The Constructor may speak; Ramsports rules.
Posture 5 — Integrity Credibility Compounds
Every well-handled crisis strengthens the media-rights and sponsorship premium. This is the F1 / FIA lesson: disputes resolved on the record, inside the sport, with external appeal only on process. Crisis is not a brand failure — mishandled crisis is. Handle every one as though the next media-rights renewal depends on it, because it does.
One-line default

"Ramsports has ruled on [matter]. Full finding and appeal path published. Player-welfare resources available via integrity office. Season competition continues on schedule."
