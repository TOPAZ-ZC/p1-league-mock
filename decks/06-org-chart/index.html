07 · Reporting Lines
PAGE 08
WHO REPORTS TO WHOM

Two reporting spines. Clean separation.

Every role reports up through one of two spines: the commercial spine (to the Investor entity) or the regulatory spine (to Ramsports). Roles never report to both. Escalation paths published in charter.

Commercial Spine

Reports up to Investor Entity

  • League Ops — Event Production → Investor Commercial Committee
  • League Ops — Broadcast → Investor Commercial Committee
  • League Ops — Sponsorship Activation → Investor Commercial Committee
  • League Ops — Content / Docuseries → Investor Commercial Committee
  • League Ops — Finance & Legal → Investor CFO / Board
  • 8 Constructor Team Presidents → Constructor Parent Brand (dotted: Investor)
Regulatory Spine

Reports up to Ramsports Board

  • 3 Stewards → Ramsports CEO → Board
  • 3 Traveling Refs → Chief Steward → CEO → Board
  • Integrity Office Director → Ramsports CEO → Board
  • Drug-Testing Program Lead → Integrity Office → CEO → Board
  • Player-Development Pipeline → Ramsports CEO (dotted: League Ops)
  • External Advisors (Rifkind / Kuhn / Chu / Mok) → Board chair
Concorde-level escalation
Disputes that span both spines (e.g., a commercial-sponsor conflict with an integrity finding) escalate to a Concorde-convened joint committee: Investor chair + Ramsports CEO + 2 Constructor delegates. Decision by majority. Reasoned finding published.
External-appeal path
Beyond Concorde joint committee, final appeal to Court of Arbitration for Sport or equivalent neutral body. Scope limited to matters of procedural integrity — not substitution of judgment on factual integrity findings. F1 / FIA analog.
The principle

Dual reporting spines prevent the single failure mode of the incumbent model — a regulator reporting up to its operator. Every role knows which chain it is on; every dispute knows which spine adjudicates it.
