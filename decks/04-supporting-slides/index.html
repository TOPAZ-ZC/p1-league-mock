Prize pool & events.

Prize Pool & Events · Q1-Q5
Q1. How many events per year?
20 stops. 4 quarterly swings of 5 events each. 16 standard + 4 Grand Slams.
Q2. What's the prize pool per event?
$1M per standard stop (2 singles × $125k + 3 doubles × $250k). $2M per Grand Slam.
Q3. What's the total annual prize pool?
$24M/yr (16 × $1M + 4 × $2M). Earlier drafts cited $28M; corrected to $24M on event-count reconciliation.
Q4. Why 20 stops?
Enough cadence to build a weekly audience; short enough to keep festival-weekend density and player travel sustainable. F1 runs 24; MotoGP 20; PGA 40+. 20 is the sweet spot for institutional-league production.
Q5. Why four Grand Slams?
Matches tennis's Slam structure, which is the most institutionally understood in racquet sports. Four seasonal peaks, four regional anchors, four media marquee events.

Ramsports / Governance.

Ramsports / Governance · Q11-Q14
Q11. Who is Ramsports?
Independent technical + integrity body. Operating business, not a vision document. Founded by Richard Gossett with Alex Yuen (ex-NBA BIG3 China) as president. Board includes Steve Kuhn (founder MLP + DUPR), Seymour Rifkind (WPF/IPTPA), Tenniel Chu (Mission Hills), Philip Mok (HKTA/ITF).
Q12. Why separate Ramsports from the Investor entity?
Because UPA/PPA's governance conflict is the single biggest incumbent failure. Fans don't trust a regulator owned by the operator. Institutional capital won't back one either. Separation = credibility = cheaper media + sponsorship.
Q13. How is Ramsports compensated?
2% of gross league revenue + 20% equity at formation (no cash outlay). The hedge-fund "2-and-20" analog — flat 2% management fee plus a 20% equity "performance" stake. Y1 opex ~$3M (3 stewards, 3 refs, drug testing, integrity office). Y1 fees: 2% × $69M league revenue = ~$1.4M.
Q14. Does Ramsports have real regulatory teeth?
Yes. Controls player registration, event sanctioning, stewardship at stops, drug-testing protocol, dispute resolution. Cannot be overruled by Investor on technical calls.

Market / Comparables.

Market / Comparables · Q19-Q22
Q19. Why pickleball and not padel?
Pickleball has the stronger measured U.S. growth signal: SFIA counts 24.3M U.S. participants in 2025 and 171.8% growth from 2022-2025. Padel remains earlier in the U.S. Learning curve 2 minutes vs padel's 20. Infrastructure cost for a pickleball court is ~$8k vs $50k+ for padel.
Q20. Why now and not 2028?
The Bartlett exponential window. 75M model → 300M active by 2032 — that 4× demand curve is compounding now. Category-defining commercial rights will be priced off the Y1 base rate. Waiting 2 years = compete with RedBird/CVC/Liberty doing this deal at 3-4× today's implied valuation.
Q21. What's the LIV Golf comparable?
LIV raised ~$3B+ on a similar category-vacuum thesis (PGA had a player-pay + format-innovation gap; LIV filled it). P1 has the same vacuum — PPA/UPA governance failure — and 10× the addressable-market runway. But P1 costs $130M not $3B, because Year 1 infrastructure doesn't require sovereign wealth scale.
Q22. What's the TGL comparable?
TGL (Tiger/McIlroy tech-driven golf league) proved that constructor-style ownership + prime-time media windows works. P1 is TGL's format IP + F1's governance stack + LIV's capital story — applied to a sport that's 10× the size of golf's addressable US base.

Operations & Risk / Mitigation.

Operations · Q23-Q26
Q23. Who runs events on the ground?
Ramsports + local promoter partner per stop. Ramsports controls sanctioning + integrity; local promoter handles gate, hospitality, sponsorship activation. 4-day load-in + operate + teardown.
Q24. What's the venue strategy?
4-8k-seat arenas. Retractable-roof preferred for weather-sensitive outdoor stops. Singapore Indoor Stadium, Coca-Cola Arena (Dubai), Moody Center (Austin), O2 Arena (London) are template targets.
Q25. What's the broadcast production model?
Host-broadcaster feed produced by Ramsports-approved vendor. Live international distribution via Sky / ESPN / DAZN / beIN + regional partners. Docuseries pipeline separate, rights sold per season to a single streaming platform.
Q26. How is the docuseries monetized?
Year-2 onward. Analog: F1 Drive to Survive (Netflix, estimated $50M/season windfall), Full Swing (PGA, Netflix), Break Point (ATP, Netflix). $15-30M/yr run-rate by Y3.
Risk / Mitigation · Q27-Q30
Q27. What's the biggest risk?
Constructor recruitment — we need 8 global brands committed at $10M each by July 1 2026. Mitigation: 4 brands already in term-sheet dialogue as of April 2026; Sideline relationship unlocks 2 more via portfolio co-investors.
Q28. What if a Constructor defaults mid-season?
Replacement protocol. License fee non-refundable once deployed. Constructor seat reverts to Ramsports → resold at open-market rate. Team roster protected via contractual transfer to replacement Constructor at book value.
Q29. What if the Olympic committee blocks the name?
P1 is a commercial entity; Olympic pathway goes through Seymour Rifkind / WPF. No naming overlap. Olympic inclusion would accelerate P1's TAM (pickleball at LA 2028?), not conflict with it.
Q30. What if a player gets banned for integrity violation?
Ramsports integrity protocol: independent drug-testing, betting-integrity monitoring, stewardship at every match. Sanctions precedent mirrors F1's. Constructor team takes reputational hit; integrity credibility compounds across the league.

RAMSPORTS INTEGRITY PROTOCOL · F1-LEVEL STANDARDS

Credibility as competitive advantage.

Institutional capital underwrites governed sports. Fan trust and sponsor value compound when the regulator is visibly independent and the integrity protocol is enforced. Ramsports is the operating engine of that posture.

3 Stewards

Independent sanctioning authority

Three Ramsports-appointed stewards rotate across the 20-stop calendar. Final authority on in-event calls. Cannot be overruled by the commercial rights holder.

3 Traveling Referees

Competition oversight at every stop

Three lead referees travel the full calendar with the tour. Consistent enforcement of competition rules across regions, venues, and surfaces. Local officials assist but do not call.

Drug Testing

WADA-aligned protocol

Independent drug-testing program with pre-event and at-event testing for all contracted pros. WADA-aligned substances list. Sanction precedent mirrors F1 / ATP.

Betting-Integrity Monitoring

Live-match surveillance

Partnership with integrity-monitoring firms (Sportradar / IBIA analog). Real-time odds surveillance across regulated markets. Immediate reporting protocol on suspicious activity.

Why integrity compounds

The incumbent (PPA/UPA/MLP) has a governance-credibility deficit that structurally caps media-rights pricing and sponsor appetite. P1 starts where the F1 post-2017 governance arrived. Credible integrity → cheaper media + sponsorship → higher Constructor economics → lower cost of capital → higher exit multiple. Every dollar spent on Ramsports integrity compounds into enterprise value.

TOP 5 OBJECTIONS · ONE-LINE REBUTTALS

The five objections most likely to surface. Each one already answered.

"Pickleball is a fad."
24.3M measured U.S. participants, 75M global model base, 300M 2032 scenario. Participation-sport brands (Athletic Brewing, Bandit Running, Urban Golf Performance) are compounding inside the same macro curve. The anti-fad argument is measured participation depth, not an unsupported fixed CAGR.
"PPA / UPA / MLP has a head start."
US-only, governance-conflicted, closed-media. Institutional capital will not back that structure. P1's vacuum thesis is exactly that the incumbent cannot be institutionalized without a full restructure — which it cannot do without dismantling its own ownership structure. That's the moat.
"$10M per Constructor seat is too high."
F1 Constructors pay $200M+ today. MLP teams trade at $25-35M domestically. TGL franchises price at $50-60M. $10M is the Y1 institutional entry price for global scarcity. Sub-$10M seats read as sponsorship, not franchise — and kill the cap-table clarity investors require.
"The calendar is too aggressive for Y1."
20 stops with 4-day load-in windows. Ramsports + local-promoter execution model. Gossett + Yuen have 20+ years of Asia sports promotion; Ramsports infrastructure is already running. F1 launches 24, MotoGP runs 20 — this is proven institutional-league cadence.
"Why not wait to confirm the curve?"
Category-defining commercial rights are priced off the Y1 base rate. Waiting 2 years = compete with RedBird / CVC / Liberty / Silver Lake doing the same deal at 3-4× today's implied valuation. The $50M Concorde escrow makes waiting strictly dominated — the downside is capped at zero, so early-entry is pure optionality.
