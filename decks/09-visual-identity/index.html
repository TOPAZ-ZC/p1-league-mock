Accent discipline

Cyan = information, wayfinding, data. Violet = brand hero moments, structural claims. Gold = capital, scarcity, premium. Never mix three accents in a single composition — pick one dominant, one support, zero decoration.

Clear-space rule

Minimum clear space around the mark or wordmark is equal to the height of the mark itself. Never stretch, rotate, or place the mark on a photographic background without a color-tinted overlay at rgba(5,6,12,0.7) or stronger.

05 · MOTION & INTERACTION

Quick. Deliberate. Functional — never theatrical.

Motion confirms causality. Hover lifts, focus rings, and state transitions resolve in 150ms with ease-out. Nothing animates in decoration — every transition is a signal.

HOVER LIFT

translateY(-1px) · 150ms

Buttons and CTAs lift one pixel on hover. Deliverable cards lift two pixels. Never more — never slower.

HOVER ME
FOCUS RING

2px cyan · 3px offset

Keyboard focus uses a 2px solid cyan outline with 3px offset and 6px radius. Visible in every theme. Never removed.

FOCUSED STATE
THEME FLIP

Instant · no transition

Theme swap is immediate — no fade, no crossfade. The toggle is a state change, not a performance. Tokens resolve in a single paint.

Motion rule

If the motion does not confirm a state change or guide the eye to the next action, cut it. Decks must read as static in print and feel responsive on screen — nothing in between.

07 · COMPARISONS (.vs)

Good / bad. Old / new. Incumbent / P1.

The .vs primitive is the house pattern for any structural comparison. Red rail marks the broken model, green rail marks the P1 answer. Use it when the decision is binary.

Old way — PPA / UPA

  • Regulator owned by the operator. Credibility collapsed.
  • Fans banned from filming. Creators capped at 2 min/day.
  • U.S.-only footprint. No global pathway.
  • Player contracts one-sided. Delayed pay. Culture of fear.

P1 way

  • Ramsports independent. Separation of regulation and commercial rights.
  • Open-media model. Fans film. Creators amplify. Docuseries pipeline.
  • 20 events, 17 countries, 4 regional swings from Year 1.
  • Transparent governance. Players with real leverage. Credibility compounds.
08 · MOBILE PARITY

Apple HIG baseline. Webflow breakpoints. Print-safe in both directions.

Every page in this system passes a mobile-screenshot gate before it ships. 44pt minimum touch target, 17pt body at mobile, clamp() on every hero, stacked grids under 900px. Theme toggle visible on every screen — dark default, light parity.

44pt
Min touch target

Every button, toggle, chip, and card CTA clears 44pt (roughly 44px CSS) on mobile. Non-negotiable.

17pt
Body at mobile

Body scales to 17px at max-width: 768px. Legibility before density.

900px
Stack breakpoint

All multi-column grids collapse to single column at 900px. Stat bars and vs-comparison too.

clamp()
Fluid display type

H1 uses clamp(2.2rem, 4.2vw, 3.4rem). Scales smooth, never brittle.

Webflow breakpoint ladder

320 / 480 / 640 / 768 / 1024 / 1280 / 1600. Hamburger nav appears under 640px. Container padding drops from 28px to 20px under 768px. No content reflow creates horizontal scroll.

09 · VOICE

Institutional. Confident. First-principles.

Write the way a senior capital allocator would want to be spoken to. Lead with claim; follow with evidence. No filler. No consultant structure. Answer first, reasoning only if load-bearing.

Do
  • "20 stops. 17 countries. $50M player pool."
  • "Escrow-protected until 8 Constructors sign."
  • "Ramsports is not a vision document. It is an operating business."
  • "Commit capital. Not attention. This is a binary."
  • Numbers spelled exactly as they appear in the financial model.
Don't
  • "We're excited to explore how P1 might potentially..."
  • "Best-in-class, category-defining, world-class..."
  • "Our team believes this represents a unique opportunity to..."
  • "Leverage synergies across the pickleball ecosystem..."
  • Round numbers that drift from the canonical financial model.
