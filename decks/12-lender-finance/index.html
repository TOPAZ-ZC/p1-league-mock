02 · Why Debt, Why Now
PAGE 03
CAPITAL EFFICIENCY AT EBITDA INFLECTION

Debt bridges the equity-expensive window.

P1's valuation compounds fastest between Y1 launch and Y3 EBITDA inflection. A senior term facility at Concorde close funds this window without issuing further equity at launch pricing — every dollar of debt today is a dollar of Series B dilution avoided in 2028.

Y1-Y2 operating window

The expensive-equity window

Revenue ramps from $69M to $96M, with EBITDA crossing positive in Y2. Pre-inflection equity is the most expensive capital in the stack. Debt sized to bridge this window costs less.

Y3 EBITDA inflection

Serviceability kicks in

25% EBITDA margin on $126M revenue = $32M EBITDA. DSCR coverage clears 5× on a $40-60M facility. Facility becomes self-amortizing.

2029-30 IPO refi

Public-market takeout

Tracking-stock IPO at $1B+ valuation refinances facility at 50-100bps tighter spread or takes it out entirely with IPO primary proceeds. 4-year effective tenor, bridge-to-public profile.

Comparable precedent

Liberty Media's F1 acquisition was partially debt-funded against contractual broadcast receivables. PGA Tour Enterprises drew senior debt against Constructor-equivalent franchise receivables. LIV Golf carried PIF-guaranteed sovereign credit facilities. Sports-IP lending is a proven sub-category — P1 fits the profile.

05 · Covenant Structure
PAGE 06
STANDARD SENIOR TERM LOAN COVENANTS

Lender protection without operational choke.

FINANCIAL · MIN DSCR

≥ 1.75× from Y3

Tested quarterly on trailing-12 EBITDA / debt service. Cure right via equity contribution within 30 days of breach. Applies only after amortization begins Y3.

FINANCIAL · MAX LEVERAGE

Debt / EBITDA ≤ 3.0× by Y3

Gradual step-down from Y1 holiday through Y3. Y4 target ≤ 2.0×. Y5 target ≤ 1.5×. Forces natural deleveraging as ramp progresses.

FINANCIAL · MIN EBITDA

Y3 ≥ $10M, Y5 ≥ $45M

Annual trailing EBITDA floors. Tested at fiscal year-end. Breach triggers consent rights on distributions, new debt, and major asset sales — not acceleration.

OPERATIONAL · CONSTRUCTOR RETENTION

≥ 6 of 8 seats active

Number of active Constructor seats cannot fall below 6. Forced sale protocol for vacated seats. Event of default if < 5 for two consecutive quarters.

NEGATIVE · DIVIDENDS

Blocked until Y4

No distributions to equity holders until amortization reduces principal below $35M. Post-Y4, permitted distributions capped at 50% of free cash flow.

NEGATIVE · NEW DEBT

No pari-passu debt

No new debt senior or pari-passu to facility without lender consent. Subordinated debt permitted up to $25M (Series B bridge paper) if DSCR > 2.5×.

STRUCTURAL · CHANGE OF CONTROL

Mandatory prepay

Change-of-control — anchor sells >50% stake or Ramsports exits — triggers mandatory prepayment at par + accrued. Tracking-stock IPO is NOT a change-of-control event.

REPORTING · CADENCE

Monthly ops · quarterly financials

Monthly operating dashboard (ticket sales, media delivery, Constructor payment status). Quarterly audited financials. Annual budget with lender consultation.

07 · Pre-Launch Failure Mode
PAGE 08
THE WORST CASE IS NOT THE LENDER'S PROBLEM

Drawdown is contingent on Concorde close.

The $50M anchor is in escrow from Concorde formation through milestone. If 8 Constructors don't sign by July 1, 2026, the anchor refunds and the facility never draws. Lender exposure begins only at confirmed $130M capital close.

FAILURE MODE A

Milestone missed · Jul 1, 2026

  • Anchor's $50M refunds via escrow release
  • Facility is not drawn — zero lender exposure
  • Ramsports formation costs borne by league entity
  • Commitment fees (if agreed) paid through Concorde capital
FAILURE MODE B

Launch executes, Y1 revenue misses

  • Y1 interest-only period absorbs negative EBITDA
  • Collateral coverage (8 licenses, paid in cash at Concorde close) exceeds principal
  • DSCR covenant doesn't apply until Y3 amortization starts
  • Cure right via equity contribution; anchor commits pro-rata if needed

SPORTS-IP LENDING PRECEDENT

P1 fits a well-understood sub-category.

Sports-IP-backed lending is a defined credit sub-category with a decade of precedent. P1's profile — contractual franchise receivables + media-rights ramp + governance-separated structure — maps cleanly to recognized comps.

F1 post-Liberty
Liberty Media acquisition debt stack ($4B+ facility, collateralized against broadcast + team receivables). Proved sports-IP collateral is bankable at institutional scale.

PIF-guaranteed sovereign credit. Different structure (state-backed), but established sports-league lending at comparable revenue stage.
$3B+ · 2022-24
UFC / TKO
Silver Lake + Endeavor debt stack against PPV / broadcast receivables. Single-sport IP with contractual media streams — closest structural analog to P1 media collateral.
$2B+ · 2023

CVC sports-infrastructure debt facility model. Multi-market sports-rights collateral; CVC operating playbook directly analogous to P1's global market structure.
$1B+ · 2021-22
The delta vs P1

Every comp above is post-launch. P1's facility is pre-launch but contractually secured by $80M of countersigned Constructor receivables prior to drawdown. Smaller facility size ($40-60M vs $1B+ comps) reflects the earlier stage — but collateral quality (non-refundable cash receivables) arguably exceeds the comps.
