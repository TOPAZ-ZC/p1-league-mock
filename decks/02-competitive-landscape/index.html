01 · The Incumbent
PAGE 02
WHO CONTROLS PROFESSIONAL PICKLEBALL TODAY

The PPA / UPA / MLP stack — and why institutional capital will not back it.

The domestic incumbent operates as a single economic entity across three legal shells: PPA (tour), UPA (regulator), MLP (team league). Each has made the incumbent less investable over time.

PPA Tour
Professional Pickleball Association
US-only · primary tour
Founded as the private-equity-backed domestic tour. Owns the player contracts, controls the event calendar, dictates media-rights terms. Fan-filming bans and 2-minute creator caps repeat F1's pre-2017 closed-media posture.
CLOSED MEDIA US-ONLY PLAYER RESENTMENT
UPA
United Pickleball Association
"Regulator" · owned by the operators
The regulator created in 2023 to replace USA Pickleball. Marketed as independent — but owned by PPA / MLP stakeholders. This is the governance-conflict failure mode: a regulator cannot be owned by the operator it regulates. Institutional capital reads this instantly.

Major League Pickleball
record $16M expansion-seat comp · team league

02 · The 4-Axis Vacuum
PAGE 03
THE INCUMBENT IS BROKEN ON FOUR AXES

P1 is built from the opposite philosophy on every axis.

Incumbent — PPA / UPA / MLP

  • Governance Conflict. UPA replaced USA Pickleball as "independent" regulator while owned by PPA / MLP. Capital will not underwrite this structure.
  • Player Resentment. Delayed payments. One-sided contracts. Minor-breach suspensions. Culture of fear. Leaks to the trade press.
  • Closed Media. Fans banned from filming. Creators capped at 2 minutes per day. Repeating F1's pre-2017 mistake — a solved problem.
  • U.S. Only. No international pro pathway. No global governance. No media framework outside North America.

P1 — Opposite Philosophy on Every Axis

  • Independent Regulator. Ramsports is a true governing body, structurally separated from the commercial rights holder. F1 / FIA pattern.
  • Player-Aligned. Transparent governance. Athletes with real leverage. Credibility becomes competitive advantage — both on recruitment and on media rights.
  • Open Media. Fans encouraged to film + share. Docuseries pipeline from Y1. Daily content quotas. Highlights syndicated globally.
  • Global on Day Zero. 20 events. 17 markets. Asia, Europe, Americas, MENA. Four swings culminating in Grand Slams; season finale is the Tour Championship.
The vacuum in one sentence

Professional pickleball has no institutional home — no F1, no ATP, no PGA. The biggest sport of the next decade is operating with a domestic promoter stack that no fund manager, sovereign, or media platform would underwrite at the valuation the sport itself warrants.

03 · LIV Golf Case Study
PAGE 04
CATEGORY-VACUUM THESIS — CAPITALIZED AT SOVEREIGN SCALE

LIV proved the vacuum thesis attracts institutional capital.

LIV Golf

Anchor: PIF (Saudi Public Investment Fund) · ~$3B+ raised · Format: 54-hole team-based · Comp: P1 structure, 1/20 the capital

What worked

  • Format innovation — 54 holes, shotgun start, team scoring created a differentiated TV product that the PGA had no answer for.
  • Franchise-team ownership — 13 teams, equity-style participation for top players attracted defectors at nine-figure guarantees.
  • Sovereign anchor — PIF capital underwrote the full launch without requiring profitability for 3+ years; institutional patience.
  • Category-vacuum exploit — PGA had a player-pay + format-innovation gap; LIV filled it and forced the PGA into structural reform.

What transfers to P1

  • Vacuum thesis is fundable — institutional capital backs category-vacuum plays when the governance structure is sound.
  • Format IP is defensible — a differentiated competitive format + team ownership is a durable moat; P1's festival-weekend + 8-Constructor stack echoes this.
  • Capital required is 1/20 of LIV — P1 launches on $130M, not $3B, because Y1 infrastructure and player economics are an order of magnitude lower in pickleball than golf.
  • Broader TAM runway — P1 has 10× the addressable-market curve LIV had; LIV's capital scale is not required to capture P1's opportunity.
The inversion

LIV paid sovereign-scale capital for a mature-sport vacuum. P1 pays Y1 pricing for a pre-institutional-sport vacuum. Same thesis, 1/20 the cost, 10× the runway.

04 · TGL Case Study
PAGE 05
TECH-DRIVEN FORMAT + CONSTRUCTOR OWNERSHIP — VALIDATED

TGL proved the team-ownership + prime-time-media combo in participation sports.

TGL — Tiger & McIlroy's Tech-Driven Golf League

Anchor: TMRW Sports + Sofi Stadium infrastructure · Network: ESPN primetime · Format: 6-team, indoor-sim + shortgame

What worked

  • Constructor-team ownership structure — 6 franchised teams with equity participation for top players; compressed-season format.
  • Prime-time TV window — Tuesday-night ESPN slot, uncontested sports inventory; 3M average viewers per night in Year 1.
  • Compressed stadium format — 90-minute matches, purpose-built indoor venue, format-first not tradition-first.
  • Athlete-owner alignment — players with real equity, real media rights, real leverage — the anti-incumbent posture.

What transfers to P1

  • Team-ownership validates the $10M Constructor license; TGL franchises price at $50-60M at Y2; P1 Y1 pricing is 5-10× undervalued relative to same-structure comp.
  • Format innovation is the moat — P1's festival-weekend + Grand Slam structure is the P1 equivalent of TGL's 90-minute sim-stadium format.
  • Prime-time networks want category-defining products — TGL proved ESPN will pay for a new league with the right shape. P1 runs the same media arc with docuseries leverage.
  • Athlete-first governance compounds — player alignment is a durable cost-of-capital advantage.
P1 = TGL's format IP + F1's governance stack + LIV's capital story

Each comparable validates one piece of the P1 stack. TGL demonstrates the format + team + primetime triangle. F1 demonstrates the governance separation. LIV demonstrates the capital thesis. P1 is the first entity that integrates all three inside a pre-institutional-sport vacuum.

05 · F1 / Liberty Transformation
PAGE 06
THE STRUCTURAL TEMPLATE P1 PORTS

Liberty Media bought F1 at ~$8B. It is now a $20B+ category.

Formula 1 under Liberty Media (2017 → today)

Anchor: Liberty Media Group · Entity shape: FIA (regulator) + FOM (commercial) + 10 Constructors · Structural unlock: open-media + geographic expansion

What worked

  • Separation of regulation from commercial rights — FIA governs, FOM commercializes. Institutional capital could underwrite one side without governance conflict in the other.
  • Open-media pivot — fans + creators filming + sharing from the paddock; Drive to Survive docuseries; short-form social media engagement replaced the closed-paddock TV model.
  • US & new-market expansion — Miami GP, Las Vegas GP, Austin GP, Madrid GP; deliberate invasion of the highest-value media markets.
  • Constructor-economics compounding — team valuations 10×-ed from 2017 to 2024; Williams, McLaren, Haas all trade at 9-figure EVs.

What P1 ports on day zero

  • Ramsports = FIA. Independent, mandated, structurally protected from commercial-rights holder.
  • Investor = Liberty. Global commercial rights holder, anchor stake, tracking-stock exit path.
  • 8 Constructors = F1 Constructors. Scarce franchise slots, global-brand franchisees, local-market revenue.
  • Open media from Y1 — P1 starts where F1 arrived after 2017; doesn't have to reverse a decade of closed-paddock culture.
The timing arbitrage

Liberty bought F1 in 2017 — a mature 70-year-old sport at scale. P1 launches on the same structure at Y1, inside a sport that is 4× its current size by 2032. The compounding window is longer and the entry pricing is an order of magnitude lower.

06 · MLP Comparison
PAGE 07
STEVE KUHN'S PLAYBOOK — SCALED TO GLOBAL

MLP validated the franchise-team model at US scale. P1 takes it global.

Major League Pickleball (MLP)

Founder: Steve Kuhn · Current expansion-seat comp: $16M · Scope: US-only · Format: franchised teams, gender-balanced roster, compressed event calendar

Format + governance learnings

  • Franchised-team model at $10M+ seat pricing proved out at domestic scale.
  • DUPR global rating system — Kuhn's parallel infrastructure, now the de facto global pickleball rating standard.
  • Gender-balanced roster format created media-friendly storytelling the PPA singles-first structure could not match.
  • Compressed calendar delivered better per-event attention than the PPA year-round grind.

What P1 inherits and extends

  • Kuhn sits on the Ramsports board. Full playbook + DUPR pipeline + team-draft mechanics flow to P1 governance on day one.
  • Franchise pricing benchmark — current MLP expansion-seat comps around $16M anchor the $10M Constructor license at a defensible floor.
  • International pathway — MLP is US-only; P1 globalizes the format across 17 markets. The one axis MLP cannot cross alone.
  • Independent-regulator upgrade — MLP operates inside the PPA-owned UPA regulatory layer; P1 replaces that with Ramsports independence.
P1 ≠ MLP competitor. P1 = MLP + globalization + independent governance.

MLP proved what works at US scale. P1 runs the same thesis on a global footprint, with a structurally separate regulator, and with open-media + docuseries economics that MLP cannot access while embedded inside the PPA stack.

07 · Tennis Comparators
PAGE 08
HOW TENNIS ORGANIZED ITSELF GLOBALLY

ATP / WTA / Grand Slam — the racquet-sport structural reference.

Tennis is the most institutionally-understood racquet sport. Its four Grand Slam structure is priced precisely by media markets, sponsors, and sovereign hosts. P1 mirrors the Slam arc — four Grand Slams per season, regional swings, one Tour Championship.

ATP Tour

Men's global circuit

60+ events across Masters 1000, 500, 250 tiers. $220M+ annual prize pool across tiers (excl. Slams). Multi-region calendar; global broadcast. P1's 20-stop calendar is the Masters-1000 tier equivalent — higher density, higher per-event prize pool.

WTA Tour

Women's global circuit

Parallel tier structure, prize-money parity at Slams. Shared-event WTA/ATP stops (Miami, Madrid, Indian Wells) run the exact festival-weekend model P1 ports. Gender-mixed format is an option, not a retrofit.

Grand Slams · AO / RG / Wimbledon / US Open

Four seasonal marquees

$50-70M prize pool per Slam. Sovereign-host relationship (Melbourne, Paris, London, New York). P1 Grand Slams — Miami, London, Singapore, Dubai — mirror the Slam arc with $2M prize pool each at Y1 (scaling to $5M+ by Y5).

ITF & governance

Independent federation layer

ITF governs Davis Cup, Billie Jean King Cup, ranking integrity. A true independent layer above the commercial tours. Ramsports occupies the ITF-equivalent slot in the P1 stack — governance insulated from commercial operators.

The institutional pattern

Tennis's four-Slam + independent-federation architecture is the most durable racquet-sport structure ever built. P1 ports it, compresses the calendar to 20 stops, and operates under a single global commercial rights holder rather than a fragmented tour system. Same institutional logic, cleaner economics.
