02 · Why P1 Media Is Different
PAGE 03
THE INCUMBENT IS BROKEN

Open-media model vs PPA's closed model.

PPA's media posture is a replay of F1's pre-2017 mistake. Fan filming banned. Creators capped at 2 minutes per day. Rights concentrated. P1 is architected on the Liberty-F1 post-2017 playbook, from Season 1.

PPA / UPA — closed model

  • Fan filming banned at courts and arenas.
  • Creators capped at 2 min/day — kills amplification loop.
  • U.S.-only rights footprint — no international currency.
  • Docuseries inventory absent — no Drive-to-Survive analog.
  • Single-buyer concentration in each market — no pricing tension.

P1 — open-media model

  • Fans encouraged to film + share. Creators amplify every stop.
  • Daily content drops from every event — 60-sec + 15-min formats.
  • 17 markets, 5 regional tiers with independent broadcasters.
  • Docuseries pipeline greenlit Y2, premieres Y3, renewal Y4.
  • Unbundled live vs docuseries vs highlights — three buyers per region.
The pattern that compounded F1

Pre-2017: F1 banned on-track filming and locked rights to a single market. Post-Liberty: open-media, unbundled rights, Drive-to-Survive. F1 valuation compounded from ~$4B to $20B+ in seven years. P1 starts in the post-2017 posture — no ten-year correction required.

10 · Bundling Strategy
PAGE 11
BUNDLE AT LAUNCH. UNBUNDLE FOR VALUE.

Y1-Y2 bundled. Y3+ unbundled.

Bundling at launch creates a single launch partner willing to invest marketing behind P1. Unbundling at maturity extracts region-specific pricing and triggers multi-buyer tension in each market. The sequence matters — unbundle too early and you lose launch-partner marketing lift; bundle too long and you cap the value ceiling.

Y1-Y2 · BUNDLED LAUNCH

One global partner

  • Global rights + regional highlight rights bundled in one package.
  • Launch partner commits to marketing spend in exchange for exclusivity.
  • Targets: Sky, ESPN+, DAZN, Amazon — multi-market platforms.
  • Pricing: $32-45M/yr — launch-partner bundled ceiling.
Y3+ · UNBUNDLED

Three products, six regions

  • Live sport · regional deals (5-6 markets).
  • Docuseries · single-streamer global license.
  • Highlights · creator-native syndication, per-platform deals.
  • Pricing: $60-82M/yr across all product lines by Y5.
Separability

Live vs docuseries vs highlights are separable from Day 1. Regional rights are separable geographically. Grand Slams are separable from standard stops. Each of those seams is a future value-extraction trigger — the league unlocks them as the property matures.
