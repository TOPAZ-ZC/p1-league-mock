Valuation arithmetic — reconciling the $250M+ implied

$50M for 80% implies entry post-money of $62.5M at Concorde signing. The structural asymmetry

Bear caps at 1.0× refund. Base pays 8×. Bull pays 14×. That is a convex payoff with a hard floor — structurally rare in institutional vehicles. The 80% entry ownership is what drives it; the 20% Ramsports grant + franchise-fee operating capital lets the anchor hold that much equity for $50M.

Why the right Constructor profile is large multi-national

The $7M/yr media halo at Y5 is only ROI-measurable for a brand that already buys category-equivalent media at $10M+/yr. That's adidas, Red Bull, Coca-Cola, Emirates, Skechers, Lululemon, Mubadala-type capital, Mission Hills Group — brands where the $10M franchise is a rounding error on their annual marketing budget but the 1 billion+ attributed impressions/yr is meaningful inventory. A mid-market consumer brand would not clear the ROI hurdle; the slot would underperform.

ANCHOR EXPOSURE

Dilution policy is the #1 MOIC driver

The anchor's ability to participate pro-rata in Y3 Series B + Y4 pre-IPO is worth ~6× to final MOIC (17.6× no-dilution vs 8.0× moderate dilution). Structure follow-on rights at Concorde formation, don't negotiate them post-hoc.

If the Constructor's brand-lift measurement firm cannot attribute impressions cleanly to P1-branded surfaces, the $22M cumulative media halo becomes unrecognized by internal marketing finance. Pre-negotiate attribution methodology at franchise signing [S11,S12].

Keep the Constructor filter tight: 8 seats, top-tier multi-national only.

The institutional takeaway

Anchor gets the equity convexity with a hard floor. Constructor gets the media halo with a franchise-appraisal tail. Ramsports earns 20% carry on both tails plus 2% ongoing revenue share. This is a cap table designed for all three parties to win on separate axes — not zero-sum.
