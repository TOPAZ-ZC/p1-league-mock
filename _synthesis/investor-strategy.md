## 5. 36 refined Q&A — frozen content (to be embedded in Deck 04)

These are the 36 highest-leverage investor questions with rehearsed answers. Source: founding conversation.

**Prize Pool & Events**

Q1. How many events per year?
A. 20 stops. 4 quarterly swings of 5 events each. 16 standard + 4 Grand Slams.

Q2. What's the prize pool per event?
A. $1M per standard stop (2 singles × $125k + 3 doubles × $250k). $2M per Grand Slam.

Q3. What's the total annual prize pool?
A. $24M/yr (16 × $1M + 4 × $2M). Earlier drafts cited $28M; corrected to $24M on event-count reconciliation.

Q4. Why 20 stops?
A. Enough cadence to build a weekly audience; short enough to keep festival-weekend density and player travel sustainable. F1 runs 24; MotoGP 20; PGA 40+. 20 is the sweet spot for institutional-league production.

Q5. Why four Grand Slams?
A. Matches tennis's Slam structure, which is the most institutionally understood in racquet sports. Four seasonal peaks, four regional anchors, four media marquee events.

**Economics**

Q6. What's the total capital at launch?
A. $130M. $50M anchor investor + 8 × $10M Constructor licenses.

Q7. Why $10M per Constructor seat?
A. Low enough that 8 global brands can commit without board escalation; high enough to create scarcity and commitment. Sub-$10M seats read as sponsorship, not franchise. F1 constructors pay $200M+ now; $10M is Year-1 institutional entry.

Q8. What does the investor get for $50M?
A. Global commercial rights holder — media, sponsorship, broadcast, licensing. Anchor stake at $250M+ implied valuation. Tracking stock 2029-30. IPO target $1B+.

Q9. What's the downside protection?
A. Full $50M returned if 8 Constructors not secured by July 1 2026. Escrow structure. Zero loss. Zero penalty.

Q10. What's the exit path?
A. Tracking-stock vehicle in 2029-30 (public float). IPO at $1B+ valuation. Long-term $10B by 2035. Secondary liquidity via private market pre-IPO.

**Ramsports / Governance**

Q11. Who is Ramsports?
A. Independent technical + integrity body. Operating business, not a vision document. Founded by Richard Gossett with Alex Yuen (ex-NBA BIG3 China) as president. Board includes Steve Kuhn (founder MLP + DUPR), Seymour Rifkind (WPF/IPTPA), Tenniel Chu (Mission Hills), Philip Mok (HKTA/ITF).

Q12. Why separate Ramsports from the Investor entity?
A. Because UPA/PPA's governance conflict is the single biggest incumbent failure. Fans don't trust a regulator owned by the operator. Institutional capital won't back one either. Separation = credibility = cheaper media + sponsorship.

Q13. How is Ramsports compensated?
A. 2% of gross league revenue + 20% equity. The hedge-fund "2-and-20" analog — flat 2% management fee plus a 20% equity "performance" stake. Y1 opex ~$3M (3 stewards, 3 refs, drug testing, integrity office).

Q14. Does Ramsports have real regulatory teeth?
A. Yes. Controls player registration, event sanctioning, stewardship at stops, drug-testing protocol, dispute resolution. Cannot be overruled by Investor on technical calls.

**Constructors / Teams**

Q15. Who are the 8 Constructors?
A. Target profile: global brands with participation-consumer gravity. Target list includes Red Bull, Monster, adidas, Skechers, Coca-Cola/Pepsi, Emirates/Qatar, a sovereign-backed MENA name, and a consumer-tech partner. First 3 closes are in active dialogue.

Q16. How many players per team?
A. 8-player roster + 1 coach per Constructor. 64 contracted pros total across the league.

Q17. How are players paid?
A. Roughly 50/50 split between team salary (Constructor-funded team feed of ~$1.9M Y5) and prize money (event-funded, $24M prize pool annual). Prize money does NOT count against Constructor salary cap.

Q18. What's the Constructor 5-year return?
A. ~2.5× cash-on-cash payback + $20-40M unmonetized media value + $40-100M Y5 equity exit = combined 5-10× MOIC on $10M license.

**Market / Comparables**

Q19. Why pickleball and not padel?
A. Pickleball is growing 50%+/yr globally vs padel's 8-12%. US leadership position (75M of global 100M+ active players). Learning curve 2 minutes vs padel's 20. Infrastructure cost for a pickleball court is ~$8k vs $50k+ for padel.

Q20. Why now and not 2028?
A. The Bartlett exponential window. 75M → 300M active by 2032 — that 4× demand curve is compounding now. Category-defining commercial rights will be priced off the Y1 base rate. Waiting 2 years = compete with RedBird/CVC/Liberty doing this deal at 3-4× today's implied valuation.

Q21. What's the LIV Golf comparable?
A. LIV raised ~$3B+ on a similar category-vacuum thesis (PGA had a player-pay + format-innovation gap; LIV filled it). P1 has the same vacuum — PPA/UPA governance failure — and 10× the addressable-market runway. But P1 costs $130M not $3B, because Year 1 infrastructure doesn't require sovereign wealth scale.

Q22. What's the TGL comparable?
A. TGL (Tiger/McIlroy tech-driven golf league) proved that constructor-style ownership + prime-time media windows works. P1 is TGL's format IP + F1's governance stack + LIV's capital story — applied to a sport that's 10× the size of golf's addressable US base.

**Operations**

Q23. Who runs events on the ground?
A. Ramsports + local promoter partner per stop. Ramsports controls sanctioning + integrity; local promoter handles gate, hospitality, sponsorship activation. 4-day load-in + operate + teardown.

Q24. What's the venue strategy?
A. 4-8k-seat arenas. Retractable-roof preferred for weather-sensitive outdoor stops. Singapore Indoor Stadium, Coca-Cola Arena (Dubai), Moody Center (Austin), O2 Arena (London) are template targets.

Q25. What's the broadcast production model?
A. Host-broadcaster feed produced by Ramsports-approved vendor. Live international distribution via Sky / ESPN / DAZN / beIN + regional partners. Docuseries pipeline separate, rights sold per season to a single streaming platform.

Q26. How is the docuseries monetized?
A. Year-2 onward. Analog: F1 Drive to Survive (Netflix, estimated $50M/season windfall), Full Swing (PGA, Netflix), Break Point (ATP, Netflix). $15-30M/yr run-rate by Y3.

**Risk / Mitigation**

Q27. What's the biggest risk?
A. Constructor recruitment — we need 8 global brands committed at $10M each by July 1 2026. Mitigation: 4 brands already in term-sheet dialogue as of April 2026; Sideline relationship unlocks 2 more via portfolio co-investors.

Q28. What if a Constructor defaults mid-season?
A. Replacement protocol. License fee non-refundable once deployed. Constructor seat reverts to Ramsports → resold at open-market rate. Team roster protected via contractual transfer to replacement Constructor at book value.

Q29. What if the Olympic committee blocks the name?
A. P1 is a commercial entity; Olympic pathway goes through Seymour Rifkind / WPF. No naming overlap. Olympic inclusion would accelerate P1's TAM (pickleball at LA 2028?), not conflict with it.

Q30. What if a player gets banned for integrity violation?
A. Ramsports integrity protocol: independent drug-testing, betting-integrity monitoring, stewardship at every match. Sanctions precedent mirrors F1's. Constructor team takes reputational hit; integrity credibility compounds across the league.

**The Ask**

Q31. What does the investor say yes to, specifically?
A. Commit $50M to escrow. Sign Concorde Agreement by April 1 2026. Governance forms immediately. Capital never deploys until 8 Constructors sign by July 1 2026. If miss: full refund.

Q32. When does the investor need to decide?
A. The sooner the better — 4 Constructor conversations are live and $50M-anchor confirmation unlocks aggressive recruitment. Practical deadline: March 15, 2026 for sign; April 1 for Concorde formalization.

Q33. What if the investor wants more than 50%?
A. Open to discussion on anchor-plus structure. Base anchor is $50M at $250M+ implied valuation. Larger checks at the same pricing negotiable up to $75M before it becomes a different structure.

Q34. What if the investor wants to syndicate?
A. Fine. Anchor + up to 2 co-anchors at same terms. Total anchor side capped at 3 LPs to protect cap-table clarity.

Q35. What's the governance seat for the anchor?
A. Board observer + commercial-rights-committee chair at close. Voting board seat converts at tracking-stock activation (2029-30).

Q36. What happens post-IPO?
A. Public tracking-stock float (2029-30 target). Anchor retains stake, secondary-liquidity path via public markets. IPO valuation $1B+ base case; $2B+ bull. Long-term $10B by 2035.
