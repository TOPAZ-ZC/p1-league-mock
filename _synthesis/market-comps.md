## Model Mapping

### Participation base: 24.3M U.S. measured; 75M global model base; 300M 2032 scenario

**Support:** SFIA's current U.S. participation count is the hard, defensible floor (S14). APP's broader "played once" methodology shows materially larger reach in the same U.S. market (S15). P1's 75M global base should therefore be described as a model base, not a verified active-player fact.

**Constraint:** The 300M 2032 case is a scenario, not a sourced consensus forecast. The deck should not describe 50%+ annual global growth as a current measured fact.

**Confidence:** High for the SFIA U.S. count; medium for broad U.S. reach; low-medium for global model base until a dedicated global participation study is sourced.

### League media rights: $22M Y1 -> $76M Y5

**Support:** TGL secured exclusive ESPN distribution before launch (S4), and LIV's non-U.S. commercial/event contract revenue reached almost $65M in 2024 (S8). F1's mature global rights and commercial model is much larger than P1's target range (S3).

**Constraint:** LIV's public broadcast-rights revenue was only about $2.7M outside the U.S. in 2024 (S8), and MLP/UPA had not yet announced a full forward-looking media deal for 2026 (S10). Therefore P1's Y1 media-rights line is a signed-package target, not a market-proven pickleball run-rate.

**Confidence:** Medium-low until at least one anchor broadcaster, streamer, or regional-rights buyer is contracted.

### Sponsorship: $20M Y1 -> $44M Y5

**Support:** TGL has a live presenting sponsor, naming-rights partner, founding partners, and official partners across relevant categories (S5, S6). LIV's public non-U.S. sponsorship revenue was about $17M in 2024 (S8), giving a nearby launch-league reference point.

**Constraint:** P1 must close true multi-category partners, not single-market activation buys. This line should not be treated as proven until a title/presenting partner and category slots are signed.

**Confidence:** Medium if the Constructor roster includes global brands; low if it skews regional or mid-market.

### Event, merchandise, docuseries: $47M combined Y5

**Support:** F1's mature business is diversified across race promotion, media, sponsorship, hospitality, licensing, and other revenue (S3). TGL's venue + ESPN + partner stack shows new formats can package live event, broadcast, and brand inventory (S4-S7).

**Constraint:** No public P1 venue agreements, host-city guarantees, or streamer docuseries term sheets have been verified. Treat the lines as forecast cases with clear proof gates.

**Confidence:** Medium for event/hospitality if the 20-stop calendar is contracted; low-medium for docuseries until a platform option exists.

### Constructor franchise value: $17.5M Y5 base; $35M-$50M bull

**Support:** MLP Palm Beach was reported at a $16M record expansion valuation (S9-S10). Cadillac's reported F1 anti-dilution fee shows mature Constructor scarcity can command a far larger entry price (S13).

**Constraint:** Current pro-pickleball comps support the $17.5M base resale better than the bull case. The $35M-$50M bull requires a P1 media-rights breakout, 8-seat scarcity, and category-leading brand demand.

**Confidence:** Medium for $17.5M; low until breakout proof for $35M-$50M.

### Sponsor media value / brand lift: $22M cumulative per Constructor

**Support:** Relo's public scoreboard shows sponsor media value is now a mainstream measurement category at scale (S11), and Nielsen's analysis ties sponsorship exposure to purchase-intent lift (S12).

**Constraint:** Media value is not cash. It must be measured by an accepted third-party attribution provider and accepted by the Constructor's internal finance/marketing teams.

**Confidence:** Medium as a measurement framework; low as finance-recognized value unless attribution is contracted at franchise signing.
