## 1. The three-body architecture

P1 is Formula 1 for pickleball. Three entities, separated by design:

| Body | F1 Analog | Role | Economics |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Investor** (anchor) | Liberty Media | Holds global commercial rights — media, sponsorship, broadcast, licensing | $50M anchor into $250M+ implied valuation at launch |
| **Ramsports** | FIA | Independent technical + integrity body. 3 stewards, 3 traveling referees, drug testing | 2% of gross league revenue + 20% equity; hedge-fund "2-and-20" analog |
| **Constructors** | F1 Constructor teams | 8 licensed franchise brands. Operate teams, develop players, drive local-market revenue | $10M license fee per seat. $80M total at close. Scarce by design. |

Separation of regulation (Ramsports) from commercial rights (Investor) is the structural pattern that unlocked F1's $20B+ valuation under Liberty. P1 ports the pattern — governance locked before a dollar deploys.

## 2. The Concorde Agreement — three-step launch

**STEP 01 — FORMATION** (on/before April 1, 2026)
- Investor commits $50M to escrow (SPAC-like structure)
- Governance entity (Ramsports + Investor covenant) formally constituted
- Zero capital deployed — held until milestone

**STEP 02 — BRAND SIGN-UP** (April 1 → July 1, 2026)
- 8 Constructor brands recruited
- $10M license fee each → $80M total
- All 8 must commit by the July 1 deadline
- Capital remains in escrow during this phase

**STEP 03 — FUNDING CLOSE** (July 1, 2026 — milestone)
- If 8 brands hit: $130M total capital deployed. $250M+ implied valuation. First event: February 2027. Tracking-stock vehicle activated.
- If 8 brands NOT hit: full $50M returned to investor. Zero loss, zero penalty, complete capital return.

**This is the investor-protection spine.** The $50M is never at market risk; it only converts to equity when the Constructor side of the cap table is proven.

## 3. Season format — 20 stops, 17 countries, 5 divisions

- **20 events/yr** across Asia, Europe, Americas, Middle East
- **Four quarterly "Swings"** — regional clusters culminating in a Grand Slam
- **5 stops per swing**: 4 standard events + 1 Grand Slam (season grand finale is the Tour Championship)
- **Festival weekends** — 4-5 day stops: pro matches, celebrity Fridays, concerts, fan zones, food markets. Mini-Super-Bowls every month.
- **Open-media model** — unlike old F1 / PPA, fans film and share everything. Creators pipeline cameras into the sport. Docuseries rights downstream.

## 4. Prize pool — $24M/yr (the corrected math)

Per standard stop:
- 2 singles events × $125k = $250k
- 3 doubles events × $250k = $750k
- **= $1M per standard stop**

Per Grand Slam:
- **= $2M per Grand Slam** (4 GS per season)

Full season:
- 16 standard stops × $1M + 4 Grand Slams × $2M = **$24M / year**

Player pool combined: **$50M+** (includes salary feed from Constructors, prize money from events, bonuses).

*(Note: earlier public drafts cited $28M; corrected to $24M after event-count reconciliation. See Q&A #3.)*

## 5. Team economics — 8-player + 1-coach cap, Constructor-paid + league prize

Each Constructor fields:
- **8 players** (cap) + **1 coach** (cap)
- Team salaries are **Constructor-paid** ($2.6M Y1 → $3.2M Y5 per team)
- Prize money is **league-funded and additive** — $24M annual pool. Does NOT count against the team salary cap
- Combined ~$50M player pool at Y5 (8 teams × $3.2M Constructor salaries + $24M league prize pool)

## 7. Why now — the vacuum thesis

The incumbent (PPA/UPA/MLP) is broken on four axes. P1 is built from the opposite philosophy on each:

| Incumbent Failure | P1 Response |
|---|---|
| **Governance conflict** — UPA replaced USA Pickleball as "independent" regulator while owned by PPA/MLP | Ramsports is a true independent body. Separation of regulation and commercial rights. |
| **Player resentment** — delayed payments, one-sided contracts, minor-breach suspensions, culture of fear | Transparent governance. Athletes with real leverage. Credibility as competitive advantage. |
| **Closed media** — fans banned from filming, creators capped at 2 min/day; repeating F1's pre-2017 mistake | Open-media model. Fans encouraged to film + share. Docuseries pipeline. Daily content quotas. Highlight syndication. |
| **U.S. only** — no professional international pathways, governance, or media frameworks | Truly global. 20 events, 17 countries across Asia, Europe, Americas, MENA from Year 1. |
